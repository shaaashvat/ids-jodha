Dataset Overview

The IDS-JODHA dataset is an observational tool for studying complex systems and a challenging benchmark for perception algorithms. Collected on live urban roads in Jodhpur, India, it captures the dense, unstructured, and heterogeneous traffic dynamics that are underrepresented in existing datasets.

Key Features:

  • ✔️ Unstructured Scenarios: High agent density, no lane discipline, diverse vehicle classes.
  • ✔️ Multi-Weather Conditions: Includes both clear weather and a supplementary rainy sequence.
  • ✔️ Synchronized Multimodality: Time-aligned 3D LiDAR scans and high-resolution camera frames.
  • ✔️ Scalable Methodology: Collected with a low-cost, portable sensor suite.

Research Applications

Robust Perception

Benchmark 3D object detection, tracking, and segmentation models in dense, occluded scenarios and adverse weather conditions that challenge state-of-the-art systems.

Behavioral Modeling

Develop and validate a new generation of behaviorally accurate trajectory prediction models for vulnerable road users and vehicles in mixed, non-lane-based traffic.

Complex Systems Science

Use dense agent trajectories to empirically test theories of traffic flow, self-organization, and criticality in a real-world, chaotic system.
